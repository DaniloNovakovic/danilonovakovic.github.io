## Intent

The current Ridge milestone proves the compact overworld loop:

- a clear summit spine
- early Relay Spire visibility
- Stampede as the first new opt-in toy
- visible world-change rewards
- Cicka and one NPC making the place feel inhabited

This document looks beyond that slice. The goal is to describe how Ridge could
grow into a denser tiny overworld with:

- *A Short Hike* warmth and summit-focus
- *Hollow Knight* shortcut relief and route compression
- *Tunic* reinterpretation secrets and "oh, wait" moments

The locked long-term world direction is **2D side-view interconnected Ridge**.
This means Hollow Knight-style topology is the structural reference, not a
top-down/isometric/3D-like pivot. The mood remains gentle and personal, and the
re-reading layer remains closer to Tunic than to a combat metroidvania.

The key rule is: expand **density**, not **size**.

## Long-Term World Thesis

Ridge should eventually feel like a **small layered mountain you can learn by
heart**.

It should not become:

- a huge metroidvania map
- a biome tour
- a top-down or isometric world unless a dedicated future spike proves the cost
  is worth it
- a puzzle-box that hides progress behind obscure logic
- a lore-heavy world that forgets Danilo is the primary audience

It should become:

- one memorable summit route
- an interconnected 2D side-view map with loops, shafts, and compressed returns
- several hidden parallel strata
- a web of satisfying shortcuts
- a place where old landmarks gain new meaning after mobility, manual, or
  Cicka discoveries
- eventual movement flavors such as wall cling and double jump, treated as
  optional or later topology payoffs before they ever become main-ending gates

Late-game, the player should be able to cross the Ridge quickly, but still feel
there are a few corners worth revisiting.

## Topology Target

The target shape is a **compact three-layer mountain**.

### 1. Main Trail Spine

Purpose: readable first playthrough route from Outskirts to Relay Spire.

Properties:

- always understandable without a map
- no precision platforming required
- landmarks visible from multiple positions
- safe return path after scene exits
- remains the emotional backbone even after shortcuts open

This layer carries the *A Short Hike* feeling of "I know where I am going,
but I keep getting pleasantly distracted."

### 2. Under-Ridge Layer

Purpose: low hidden routes, cat logic, service passages, and re-seeing secrets.

Possible spaces:

- crawlspaces under paper folds
- desk undersides
- printer guts and jam paths
- service duct connectors
- torn-page gaps that look decorative until later

This layer should feel discovered, not assigned. It is where Cicka and manual
knowledge can make old spaces feel different.

### 3. High-Ledge Layer

Purpose: outlooks, glide lines, optional mobility elegance, and late-game route
compression.

Possible spaces:

- shelf lips
- taped sign roofs
- cliff notches
- exposed Relay approach ledges
- tiny lookout perches near landmark silhouettes

This layer should feel rewarding, airy, and readable rather than punishing.

## Landmark Network

A long-term Ridge does not need many zones. It needs strong anchors.

Target: **8-12 major landmarks** with 1-2 smaller connective curiosities each.

Likely landmark set:

1. **Outskirts / Trailhead**
   Current street edge, current-world bridge, and first orientation beat.
2. **Glasses Moment**
   First "re-read the world" unlock that teaches marginal reality changes.
3. **Cicka First Perch**
   Early residency signal and first hint that notice matters.
4. **Stampede Picnic Blanket Meadow**
   Friendly social node with chaos nearby.
5. **Printer Bluff**
   Manual scraps, prophecy joke energy, and under-route opportunities.
6. **Binder Clip Pass**
   A memorable choke point that later becomes easier or faster.
7. **Coffee-Ring Overlook**
   A scenic loop node that feels personal rather than epic.
8. **Domino Desk / Service Elevator**
   Deterministic puzzle hook and major route-compression unlock.
9. **Telegraph Terrace**
   Visible sparring landmark with rhythmic identity.
10. **Potassium Hint Site**
   Secret-adjacent acknowledgement without turning the Ridge into a second
   ricochet zone.
11. **High Shelf Outlook**
   Late mobility payoff node.
12. **Relay Spire / Contact Peak**
   Final destination, visible early and emotionally legible throughout.

## Shortcut Vocabulary

Shortcuts should feel handmade and world-specific, not like abstract unlock
gates.

Preferred shortcut forms:

- patched plank or taped bridge
- binder clip latch
- service elevator permit
- lowered rope or paper strip ladder
- hatch opened from the far side
- sticker-marked safe drop
- cat-discovered squeeze route
- desk object pushed into a stable crossing

Target: **5-7 meaningful shortcuts** in the mature Ridge.

Good shortcut behavior:

- shortens return time in a way the player can immediately feel
- preserves place identity instead of bypassing the whole map
- makes the mountain fold back on itself
- often opens after a mini-game clear or mobility insight

Bad shortcut behavior:

- teleports that erase spatial understanding
- fake complexity where every route is equally good
- one-use novelty that the player never values again

## Secret Types

Secrets should be **legible in retrospect**. The player should think "I saw
that before" more often than "how was I supposed to know?"

Preferred secret families:

### Manual Reinterpretation Secrets

The player reads a one-screen page and suddenly understands:

- a sign's missing meaning
- how to read a route marker
- why a harmless prop was actually a clue
- how the Relay gate is supposed to be understood

### Cicka Notice Secrets

The player follows:

- scent marks
- paw-print annotations
- suspicious pauses or perch direction
- tiny under-route hints that only make sense after the daydream

### Mobility Recontextualization Secrets

The player gains a glide pip or route confidence and can now:

- cross a gap that felt decorative before
- chain a drop into a high return ledge
- notice that a "background" shelf is actually reachable

### Sticker Memory Secrets

The world change itself becomes the clue:

- a new sticker covers a hazard and reveals a foothold
- a repaired prop creates a silhouette that suggests a route
- a margin note points at an old familiar object with new meaning

### Potassium Adjacency Secrets

The Ridge may acknowledge Potassium, but should not duplicate Potassium's fun.

Good uses:

- banana-law signage
- rebound diagrams as jokes
- NPC commentary
- major-proof recognition through the Circuit

Avoid:

- second ricochet spaces
- generic arcade prop clusters near the main route

## Progression Shape

The mature Ridge should probably use **three mobility/knowledge states**, not a
large upgrade ladder.

### State 1: First Read

- main trail is dominant
- secrets are mostly visual curiosities
- Relay Spire feels far but readable
- the world is calm and low-cortisol

### State 2: Re-Read

- Glasses, Manual Pages, and early stamps change interpretation
- first shortcuts open
- Cicka hints become more meaningful
- the player starts building a mental map of side strata

### State 3: Confident Return

- route compression becomes satisfying
- high ledges and under-ridge connectors form loops
- backtracking feels elegant rather than slow
- the mountain starts to feel "known"

This should create the feeling of growth without turning Ridge into a giant
ability gate checklist.

## Scope Guardrails

To protect the Summit direction, future expansion should still obey:

- one Ridge, many returns
- low-cortisol overworld, high-spice mini-games
- tiny cast, not RPG town simulation
- no separate biomes in v1-style expansion
- no secret logic that requires spreadsheet energy
- no mandatory precision platforming on the main route

If a future idea makes the Ridge broader but not denser, it is probably drift.

## Good Future Questions

These are worth asking when the current milestone is stable:

1. Which landmarks deserve true loopbacks, and which should stay mostly linear?
2. How many shortcuts are enough before the mountain loses its climb feeling?
3. Should Cicka's layer stay mostly optional, or should one required late-game
   route use cat logic lightly?
4. Which manual-page insights are route-changing versus merely flavorful?
5. Where should the first "I cannot get there yet" ledges appear so they build
   curiosity without frustration?

## Recommended Use

Use this document when:

- planning post-milestone Ridge expansion
- sketching landmark boards or route thumbnails
- evaluating whether a new secret or shortcut fits the Ridge dream
- deciding whether a proposed area adds density or just adds size

Do not use this document as:

- a promise that all landmarks or layers will ship
- a replacement for the active milestone plan
- justification for inflating the current Ridge slice before the loop is proven
