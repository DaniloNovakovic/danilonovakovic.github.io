# Agent B Proposal: Pocket Systems Mountain

## Pillars

- **Tiny hub, rich returns**: a compact A Short Hike-like side-view hub with cozy wandering, NPC jokes, and shortcuts.
- **One strong toy per mini-game**: one core verb, one pressure system, one reward layer.
- **Systems without sprawl**: borrow deterministic surfaces, tags, fallback paths, and systemic play without building a giant simulation.
- **Knowledge is progression**: some gates open because the player learns a clue, pattern, command, or interaction.
- **Gameplay first**: simple visuals are fine if controls and retry energy are sharp.

## Overworld Loop

The overworld becomes a portfolio hike through a hand-drawn street/ridge with rooftops, basement entrances, hobby rooms, secret props, characters, overlays, clues, and mini-game entrances.

The loop is: wander, notice a suspicious prop or clue, enter a scene or overlay, complete a short challenge, earn an item or knowledge unlock, return with one more place making sense.

## Progression

- **Glasses** reveal secret entrances and manual-page clues.
- **Circuit** from Potassium Slip powers the Basement terminal as launcher and hint archive.
- **Feather Pins** grant higher jump, wall-bounce, glide/fall-float, or sprint burst.
- **Manual Pages** teach hidden interactions.
- **Hobby Stamps** mark mini-game completion and unlock a final contact/credits path.

## Mini-Game Concepts

- **Potassium Slip**: existing flagship ricochet run. Future improvement: Circuit unlocks a basement fusion glossary rather than adding more run complexity.
- **Muay Thai: Round 33**: reactive turn fight against `TODO: AI` with Jab, Teep, Low Kick, Guard, Clinch, timed defense, Flow, and a knee finisher.
- **Deadline Garden**: 5-minute top-down survival against emails, calendar pings, and Zoom icons. Player only moves while habits auto-fire.
- **Paint Alchemy**: art mini-game using red, blue, yellow, and black surfaces with small deterministic combos.
- **Bassline Builder**: rhythm puzzle where short beat loops open doors, with clues hidden in overworld scribbles or terminal hints.
- **Dance Dash**: cozy movement challenge using slides, hops, spins, and wall-bounces.
- **Basement Barrelmancer**: 5x5 deterministic puzzle-combat using boxes, bugs, firewalls, coffee, and rubber ducks.

## Mobile Controls

Each scene gets the simplest action map: drag/release for Potassium, three large buttons for Muay Thai, drag-to-move for survivor, tap/drag for paint, four pads for music, swipe/tap for dance, and tap object plus tap destination for grid puzzles.

## Risks

- Mini-games could become shallow: prototype one core verb before expanding.
- Systemic mechanics can explode: each game gets a strict rule budget.
- Mobile can ruin good ideas: keep each concept to 1-4 large actions.
- Progression can become checklisty: lean on secrets, clues, and movement rewards.

## Fun Thesis

The design makes the portfolio feel like a toy box with memory. Potassium anchors deep "one more run" play while each additional game captures a different flavor: reactive timing, escalation, tiny systems, rhythm discovery, and cozy movement.
