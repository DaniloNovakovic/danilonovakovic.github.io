# Judging Rubric

Score each proposal out of 100.

| Category | Points | What Good Looks Like |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Fun core clarity | 20 | The proposal explains why the overworld and mini-games are fun as mechanics, not just as references. |
| Indie scope control | 20 | The plan can be built incrementally and avoids cloning entire genres. |
| Architecture fit | 20 | It uses current Scenes, Overlays, Triggers, bridge state, and scene-owned runtime patterns. |
| Mobile friendliness | 15 | Touch controls are designed per mini-game and avoid keyboard-only or precision-only requirements. |
| Personality/theme fit | 15 | The game expresses hobbies, humor, portfolio identity, and the Digital Sketchbook tone. |
| Incremental delivery | 10 | The proposal names a sensible first slice and avoids all-or-nothing delivery. |

## Judge Notes

For each proposal, write:

- strongest idea
- biggest risk
- what to steal for synthesis
- what to reject or defer

The winner should be the proposal that best supports a coherent shippable direction. The final synthesis can still steal excellent ideas from losing proposals.
