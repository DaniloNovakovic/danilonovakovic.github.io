# Potassium Slip: Mini-Game Manual

## Overview
**Potassium Slip** is a tall ricochet arena about launching one deeply irresponsible banana through a narrow office panic tube. It serves as the first secret mini-game and unlocks the **Circuit** item for a later Old TV / Ball x Pit style secret.

## The Goal
Drag toward a target, release, and bounce the main banana through staged waves of enemies. Defeat the **Potassium Compliance Officer** boss wave to acquire the Circuit.

## Controls
- **Desktop:** Click near the banana, drag toward the target, and release.
- **Mobile:** Touch near the banana, drag toward the target, and release.
- **Recall:** Hold anywhere while the banana is flying to yo-yo it back toward the launch pad.
- **Relaunch:** Keep holding as the banana returns to the launch pad, drag, and release again.
- **Return:** Press `E` or tap from the win/game-over screen.

## Waves
Potassium teaches one piece of chaos at a time:

1. **Bug Orientation:** Intern Bugs only.
2. **Scope Creep:** Introduces multi-hit Scope Blobs.
3. **Banana Split Training:** Guarantees the Banana Split pickup.
4. **Meeting Brick Wall:** Introduces Meeting Bricks and Peel Bomb.
5. **Deadline Uplink:** Introduces Deadline Drones and Bruise Cloud.
6. **Office Blender:** Mixes enemies with Rubber Rind.
7. **Magnetized Nonsense:** Adds Lunchbox Magnet.
8. **Compliance Review:** Boss wave.

## Enemies
- **Intern Bug:** 1 HP, small and quick.
- **Scope Blob:** 2 HP, drifting and annoying.
- **Meeting Brick:** 3 HP, slow and chunky.
- **Deadline Drone:** 1 HP, flies toward the top panic line.
- **Potassium Compliance Officer:** Boss enemy that drops the Circuit on defeat.

## Upgrades
- **Banana Split:** Spawns temporary tiny bouncing banana clones.
- **Bruise Cloud:** Leaves poison puddles behind the moving banana.
- **Peel Bomb:** Arms the next banana hit with an explosion.
- **Rubber Rind:** Makes wall bounces faster and sillier.
- **Lunchbox Magnet:** Pulls pickups toward the banana.

## Presentation
Potassium uses a dedicated tall board presentation. The global Phaser canvas stays fixed, but the outer React shell is portrait-shaped and the playable arena uses the central vertical slice directly, avoiding the old board-inside-board look.
